# Inductive Biases in Deep Learning

This curated space serves as a systematic reading path to master how domain knowledge, structural constraints, and physical laws are hardcoded into modern neural network architectures.

> *"An unconstrained neural network is just an expensive way to overfit your training data."* >
>
> This repository compiles the foundational, classic, and recent state-of-the-art papers that prove how introducing the right **inductive bias** can dramatically improve sample efficiency, out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization, and interpretability.

## 📂 Repository Structure

The repository is organized into numbered folders corresponding to each core research topic. Every folder contains the PDF files of a foundational **Survey/Framework** paper (to build a global mental map) and a **Landmark/Recent Method** paper (to dive into the technical implementation).

## 🔍 Detailed Breakdown per Topic

### 01. Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINN)

- **Inductive Bias:** Embedding exact physical boundaries, partial differential equations (PDEs), and conservation laws.
- **Papers:**
   - *Survey:* Karniadakis et al. (2021) – [Physics-informed machine learning](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42254-021-00314-5)
   - *Method:* Raissi et al. (2019) – [Physics-informed neural networks: A deep learning framework...](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0021999118307125)

### 02. Disentangled Representation Learning

- **Inductive Bias:** Assuming the real world is generated by independent, explanatory latent factors.
- **Papers:**
   - *Method:* Higgins et al. (2017) – [\(\beta\)-VAE: Learning Basic Visual Concepts...](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Sy2fzU9gl)
   - *Critique:* Locatello et al. (2019) – [Challenging Common Assumptions in the Unsupervised Learning...](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v97/locatello19a.html)

### 03. Causal Representation Learning

- **Inductive Bias:** Independent causal mechanisms; structural causal models that remain invariant under localized interventions.
- **Papers:**
   - *Framework:* Schölkopf et al. (2021) – [Toward(s) Causal Representation Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.11107)
   - *Survey:* Komanduri et al. (2023/2024) – [From Identifiable Causal Representations to Controllable Counterfactual Generation...](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11011)

### 04. Geometric Deep Learning & Equivariant NNs

- **Inductive Bias:** Group symmetries (translation, rotation, scale) where network transformations commute with group actions.
- **Papers:**
   - *Proto-Book:* Bronstein et al. (2021) – [Geometric Deep Learning: Grids, Groups, Graphs, Geodesics, and Gauges](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.13478)
   - *Method:* Cohen & Welling (2016) – [Group Equivariant Convolutional Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.07576)

### 05. Graph Neural Networks (GNN)

- **Inductive Bias:** Permutation invariance and relational inductive bias over non-Euclidean topological spaces.
- **Papers:**
   - *Survey:* Wu et al. (2020/2021) – [A Comprehensive Survey on Graph Neural Networks](https://doi.org/10.1109/TNNLS.2020.2978386)
   - *Classic:* Kipf & Welling (2017) – [Semi-Supervised Classification with Graph Convolutional Networks](https://openreview.net/forum?id=SJU4ayYgl)

### 06. Domain Generalization & Invariant Risk Minimization (IRM)

- **Inductive Bias:** Desired features must exhibit stable, invariant correlation patterns across distinct training environments.
- **Papers:**
   - *Method:* Arjovsky et al. (2019) – [Invariant Risk Minimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.02893)
   - *Survey:* Wang et al. (2021/2023) – [Generalizing to Unseen Domains: A Survey on Domain Generalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03097)

### 07. Deep Unfolding / Algorithm Unrolling

- **Inductive Bias:** Mapping iterative step-by-step optimization algorithms (e.g., sparse coding, wavelets) directly into structured, layer-wise neural architectures.
- **Papers:**
   - *Survey:* Monga et al. (2021) – [Algorithm Unrolling: Interpretable, Efficient Deep Learning for Signal and Image Processing](https://signalprocessingsociety.org/publications-resources/ieee-signal-processing-magazine/2021/03)
   - *Classic:* Gregor & LeCun (2010) – [Learning Fast Approximations of Sparse Coding](https://icml.cc/Conferences/2010/papers/449.pdf)

### 08. Differentiable Programming & Simulators

- **Inductive Bias:** Forcing the network to interact directly with continuous computational graphs that represent real forward measurement systems or mechanics.
- **Papers:**
   - *Survey:* Baydin et al. (2018) – [Automatic Differentiation in Machine Learning: a Survey](https://jmlr.org/papers/v18/17-468.html)
   - *Method:* Hu et al. (2020) – [DiffTaichi: Differentiable Programming for Physical Simulation](https://openreview.net/forum?id=B1eB5xSFvr)

### 09. Neuro-Symbolic AI

- **Inductive Bias:** Explicit logic gates, discrete symbol manipulation, and rule-based system execution.
- **Papers:**
   - *Survey:* Besold et al. (2017) – [Neural-Symbolic Learning and Reasoning: A Survey and Interpretation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.03902)
   - *Survey:* De Raedt et al. (2020) – [From Statistical Relational to Neuro-Symbolic Artificial Intelligence](https://www.ijcai.org/proceedings/2020/688)

### 10. Knowledge-Enhanced Learning

- **Inductive Bias:** Incorporating graph-structured fact repositories, schema constraints, and entity dependencies.
- **Papers:**
   - *Survey:* Ji et al. (2021/2022) – [A Survey on Knowledge Graphs: Representation, Acquisition, and Applications](https://researchportal.helsinki.fi/en/publications/a-survey-on-knowledge-graphs-representation-acquisition-and-appli/)
   - *Survey:* Wang et al. (2017) – [Knowledge Graph Embedding: A Survey of Approaches and Applications](https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/tk/2017/12/08047276/13rRUyoPSPy)

### 11. Bayesian Neural Networks (BNN)

- **Inductive Bias:** Parameters are represented as entire probability distributions governed by architectural priors instead of deterministic point estimates.
- **Papers:**
   - *Monograph:* Neal (1996) – [Bayesian Learning for Neural Networks](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4612-0745-0)
   - *Tutorial:* Jospin et al. (2022) – [Hands-On Bayesian Neural Networks—A Tutorial for Deep Learning Users](https://research.monash.edu/en/publications/hands-on-bayesian-neural-networks-a-tutorial-for-deep-learning-us)

### 12. Energy-Based Models (EBM)

- **Inductive Bias:** Modeling a single scalar energy value where desired configurations sit at the absolute basin of lowest energy.
- **Papers:**
   - *Tutorial:* LeCun et al. (2006) – [A Tutorial on Energy-Based Learning](https://yann.lecun.com/exdb/publis/pdf/lecun-06.pdf)
   - *Method:* Du & Mordatch (2019) – [Implicit Generation and Modeling with Energy Based Models](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/8619-implicit-generation-and-modeling-with-energy-based-models)
